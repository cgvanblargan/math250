    1.4.8.4.

    Answer.
    \begin{equation*} y = \pm\frac{1}{\sqrt{-(9x^2 + C)}} \end{equation*}
    and
    \begin{equation*} y = 0 \end{equation*}
    Remember the general solution needs to include both any equilibrium solutions together with all solutions found from separation of variables.
    🔗
    🔗

    1.4.8.5.

    1.4.8.5.a
    Answer.
    Equilibrium: \(y = 3\text{.}\) This together with separation of variables yields the general solution:
    \begin{equation*} y = 3 - \dfrac{1}{x^2/2 + C} \text{ and } y=3\text{.} \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
    1.4.8.5.b
    Answer.
    \(y = 3\) (the equilibrium solution).
    🔗
    🔗
    1.4.8.5.c
    Answer.
    \begin{equation*} y = 3 - \frac{2}{x^2 - 1} \end{equation*}
    🔗
