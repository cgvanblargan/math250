    Section 1.3 What is a Differential Equation?

    Modelling situations that are changing
    🔗
    Derivatives are used to model changing situations. Things are changing all around us.
    🔗
    Guided Question. Come up with an equation that models the following situation: The rate of change of a population P(t) is proportional to the current population.
    🔗

    Definition 1.3.1. Differential Equation.

    A differential equation is an equation involving a function, its derivatives, and the independent variable.
    🔗
    The order of a differential equation is the highest derivative appearing.
    🔗
    🔗

    Checkpoint 1.3.2.

    A simple model assumes:
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} dP/dt = kP \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Find the general solution.
    🔗
    Solution.
    Step 1: Separate variables
    🔗
    Hint 1.
    Divide both sides by P to isolate variables.
    🔗
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} dP/P = k dt \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Step 2: Integrate both sides
    🔗
    Hint 2.
    Use that ∫1/P dP = ln|P|.
    🔗
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \ln|P| = kt + C \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Step 3: Solve for P
    🔗
    Hint 3.
    Exponentiate both sides and absorb constants.
    🔗
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} P(t) = Ce^{kt} \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.3.3. Initial Conditions.

    Solve:
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} dP/dt = 2P, \quad P(0)=200 \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Solution.
    Step 1: Solve differential equation
    🔗
    Hint 1.
    This is the same form as exponential growth.
    🔗
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} P(t) = Ce^{2t} \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Step 2: Apply initial condition
    🔗
    Hint 2.
    Substitute t = 0 and P(0) = 200.
    🔗
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} 200 = C \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Final answer
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} P(t) = 200e^{2t} \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.3.4. Setting up Differential Equations.

    1. Velocity v changes at a rate proportional to v³.
      Solution.
      \begin{equation*} dv/dt = kv^3 \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Newton’s Law of Cooling.
      Solution.
      \begin{equation*} dT/dt = k(T - A) \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Slope equals sqrt(xy).
      Solution.
      \begin{equation*} dy/dx = \sqrt{xy} \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    Example 1.3.5. Verifying Solutions.

    Verify:
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} y(x) = Ce^{-x^3}, \quad y' + 3x^2 y = 0 \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Solution.
    Step 1: Differentiate
    🔗
    Hint 1.
    Use chain rule on exponential function.
    🔗
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} y' = -3x^2 Ce^{-x^3} \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Step 2: Substitute into equation
    🔗
    Hint 2.
    Replace y and y’ into LHS.
    🔗
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} y' + 3x^2 y = -3x^2 Ce^{-x^3} + 3x^2 Ce^{-x^3} \end{equation*}
    🔗
    Step 3: Simplify
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} = 0 \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Checkpoint 1.3.6. Practice.

    1. Verify y(x)=tan(x^3+C) solves y’ = 3x^2(y^2+1).
      Solution.
      Step 1: Differentiate
      🔗
      Hint 1.
      Use chain rule with tan(u).
      🔗
      🔗
      \begin{equation*} y' = sec^2(x^3+C)\cdot 3x^2 \end{equation*}
      🔗
      Step 2: Use identity
      🔗
      Hint 2.
      sec^2(u) = 1 + tan^2(u)
      🔗
      🔗
      \begin{equation*} y' = 3x^2(1 + y^2) \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Is e^x a solution to y’’ = -4y?
      Solution.
      Step 1: Compute derivatives
      🔗
      \begin{equation*} y = e^x, \quad y'' = e^x \end{equation*}
      🔗
      Step 2: Substitute
      🔗
      \begin{equation*} e^x \neq -4e^x \end{equation*}
      🔗
      Conclusion: No
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Find r such that e^{rt} solves y’’ + 5y’ + 6y = 0.
      Solution.
      Step 1: Substitute
      🔗
      \begin{equation*} r^2 e^{rt} + 5r e^{rt} + 6 e^{rt} = 0 \end{equation*}
      🔗
      Step 2: Factor
      🔗
      \begin{equation*} r^2 + 5r + 6 = 0 \end{equation*}
      🔗
      Step 3: Solve
      🔗
      \begin{equation*} (r+2)(r+3)=0 \end{equation*}
      🔗
      Final answer: r = -2, -3
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
